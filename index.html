Proof, not promises

The churn project I'm most proud of

I'd been doing retention work operationally since joining, but in March 2024 I took full ownership of it and inherited a churn rate sitting at 26%. Six months later it was 13%. Nothing magic: segmentation, early-warning alerts, and actually calling people before they were angry.

How I work

My approach to a book of business

1

Segment before anything

Not every account deserves the same motion. I split the book by value, risk and lifecycle stage first, so effort goes where it changes the outcome.

2

Automate the routine, keep the human for the hard parts

Playbooks and alerts in ChurnZero handle the predictable. My calendar is reserved for at-risk conversations and growth conversations.

3

Report what the business cares about

Adoption metrics are only useful when they connect to the customer's outcomes and our NRR. If a health score doesn't predict renewal, I rebuild it.
